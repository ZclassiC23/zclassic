The commitment-audit cure (this session) decoupled a UTXO-projection heuristic
from `tip_finalize`, so the live node unwedged from H\*=3164075 and **climbed 406
blocks to 3164482**. That exposed a deeper, pre-existing hole the commitment wedge

- The missing coin was **created at block 3164371** (post-seed; seed/delta horizon = 3155843).
- `coin_backfill` (the auto-repair owner; live `owner_ack=true` via env `ZCL_REDUCER_COIN_BACKFILL_ACK=1`) refused it in the shipped binary at the old **`creator_in_delta_window`** gate (`app/jobs/src/stage_repair_coin_backfill.c:198`). Fresh evidence on 2026-06-29 showed the creator is active-chain indexed at 3164371, the coin is absent from `coins_kv`/`created_outputs`, and `node.db.tx_inputs` records no spend before the terminal 3164483 spend. The delta horizon is therefore not a consensus refusal boundary; the repair may re-derive from the active-chain creator only after the terminal-bound no-spend scan proves clean.
- A copied datadir also exposed a stale durable `coin_backfill.refused... = txindex_miss` marker while `node.db` projection rows disagreed with the active-chain hash. The repair now treats `node.db` txindex as a hint: on txindex miss it scans a bounded 32K-block active-chain window for the creator before accepting the miss as terminal. Legacy unversioned `txindex_miss` markers are deleted and re-proven; newly proven misses persist as `txindex_miss:v2`.

## What was tried and REVERTED

Relaxing the `creator_in_delta_window` gate to re-derive the coin from its
validated creator block (3164371) exposed two deeper blockers:

1. coin_backfill's G9 no-spend scan returned **`refused_spent`** before proving
   terminal linkage to the hole block hash. That was unsafe because a fork/reorg
   race can make an off-branch spend look active for one height. The fix is to
   defer `COIN_SCAN_SPENT_FOUND` until the scan also proves `hash(H) ==
   hole_hash`; on terminal mismatch the result is `CHAIN_REBOUND`, not a durable
   spent refusal.
2. old durable `txindex_miss` markers could freeze a repaired binary before it
   rechecked active-chain block data. The fix is `txindex_miss:v2` plus legacy
   marker re-proof.

## The question that was answered

A coin cannot be spent twice on a valid chain, yet this coin is **both missing
from `coins_kv` AND appears spent** before the block (3164483) that needs it. So
one of these is true:

1. **The coin is genuinely unspent** and the no-spend scan false-positives (e.g.
   it counts a spend in an off-active-chain / orphan block). → Fix the no-spend
   scan (`app/jobs/src/stage_repair_coin_backfill_scan.c`) to count a spend only
   after terminal linkage to the hole hash, then the re-derivation path repairs
   the hole.
2. **The coin is genuinely spent** on this node's chain view, but block 3164483
   (canonical, most-work) references it. → The node's chain view at 3164483 is
   inconsistent (a reorg/fork-view or torn borrowed-snapshot artifact). → Deeper
   coins/chain-view repair; likely the sovereign-refold cutover is the real cure.

**START HERE:** compare this node's view of `dabd990f...:vin4`'s prevout against
`zclassicd` (the consensus reference, kept running):
- find the prevout outpoint: `getrawtransaction dabd990f... 1` → `vin[4].{txid,vout}`;
- ask zclassicd whether that outpoint is unspent at 3164482 (`gettxout txid vout`),
  and which block (if any) spent it.
That single check decides scan-bug (case 1) vs chain-view-corruption (case 2).

2026-06-29 copy proof answered this without a usable `zclassicd` oracle
(`zclassicd` was stuck in warmup at height 0): the stale refusal marker and
pre-terminal scan/projection behavior were sufficient to reproduce the live
failure, and the repaired binary made H\* climb on the copied datadir.
